How to Play

Gospel Jabber is a word or phrase guessing party game, similar to Hasbro's Catch Phrase, with a Latter-Day Saint theme. You will need at least two players (and ideally a large group), a phone or other mobile device to pass around the group, and a means of keeping score, if desired.

During play, one person, holding the device, gives verbal clues to their team to get them to guess the secret, randomly-chosen phrase. If their team correctly guesses the phrase before the timer expires, then they pass the device to another person; this repeats until time runs out.

Traditional rules

Players divide into two even teams, and sit in a circle; with seats alternating between members of the two teams.

One player chooses a category and starts the game. The timer will begin, and a random phrase is displayed.

The player gives verbal clues to their team mates, who try to guess the phrase. Clues that contain the word or phrase, or parts of the phrase, are not allowed. For example, if the phrase was “swimming pool”, then the clue “a place where people swim” is not allowed; neither are clues like “it rhymes with tool,” or “it starts with an ‘S.’” If the clue-giver gives a clue that is not permitted, then they must skip that phrase and start over on a new one.

If their team mates correctly guess the phrase, then the player passes the device to a member of the opposing team, who presses the green “next” button to advance to the next phrase and starts giving clues to their team. Play continues, alternating between teams, until time runs out.

When the timer expires, a buzzer will sound and a message will be displayed on-screen. The team that was guessing the phrase when time expires loses the round, and the opposing team is awarded one point. If the timer expires while the device is being passed from one person to the next, then no points are awarded.

Repeat until one team has reached a pre-determined number of points; such as five or ten points.

Variations

A second game mode, “casual,” is available in the options menu. In casual mode, the timer is disabled. This mode is suitable for a non-competitive activity.

The game lends itself to adaptation and house rules. One common variation is to permit the clue-giver to skip one (or more) phrases if they cannot describe the selected phrase; this can make the game more fun for people that don't recognize some of the names or places that are selected.

Using this app

There are several potential categories that can be selected on the start page. The “Everything” category has all of the phrases from every category. The "Children's list" category contains a subset of terms from the various categories, picked to be more suitable for younger or less-experienced players.

Scorekeeping is not built into the app, so if you want to keep score you will need to do that separately.

This app can be installed to the home screen on most Android or Apple mobile devices. Select the “Add to home screen” option on the start page for instructions.
